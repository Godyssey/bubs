STRAWBERRY GARDEN

WATASHI NO AINOTAMENI

DAY 1

I can admit that I never expected to go through what I did; to become who
I did without you. I know that I'm truly the worst at words but I'm going to try anyway.

I never thought I’d find a connection as sweet as ours,
Like discovering the first ripe strawberry in spring.
It’s no exaggeration especially given my withered seasons
hat I cherished our moments, even before our words began to bloom.
You were something rare, rich, and fulfilling; A sweetness I hadn’t tasted in so long.
The joy of sharing you with others felt like tending a secret garden,
Something beautiful I was proud to nurture.
So thank you, for all that you are and all we grew together.
I’ll never forget the roots we planted, our beginning or our end—
For you are forever entwined in me,
And you’ll always belong to this heart, my ever-blooming strawberry garden.

DAY 135

I felt indifferent to the withering of us, to the falling of our fruit.
I hated you, hated us. Yet, I longed for you, longed for us.
More than anything, I despised myself, but I loathed this barren field
where everything once flourished and where I now missed the sweetness just as much
as I mourned it.

I resented the
  way you neglected the garden we grew.
  way you left our love to die.
  how much pain your absence sowed.
  how much I let myself endure.
  day you walked away.
  emptiness left behind.
  how deeply I cared for you.
  how much I yearned for your care in return.
I longed for the
  bloom of being with you.
  joy of being us.
  bond we once cultivated.
  laughter that danced among our vines.
  conversations that flowed like streams.
  moments we shared in sunlight.
  way you made my heart blossom.
  memories we planted together.

I longed for resented loved you more than words could capture. All I wanted was you.

DAY 240

Seeing you again in our once treasured yet withering garden made my heart stop.
I couldn't possibly grasp why we were conversing now but I didn't despise it either.
Yet in my interest to mend the bond that was broken, the resentment I held surfaced once more.
It was fiery and seething,
I was livid and anxious.
But you were still and understanding.
Like a tree in the wind, you listened to my woes and unlike the rest, understood the root of my pain.
I was.. stubborn, aggressive and a little bit cagey but you still came back.. you still stayed.. with me.

We sat and planted new memories with stronger vines to craddle our laughter and intimate language
trickling through sprinklers. We rebuilt this garden from the ground up and I'd do it again if only
to be with you. And though we kept distance for quite some time, I'd never stop yearning,
longing for your return. I miss you, I love you.
I love you.
